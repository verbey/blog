Perfect musical albums

Verbiturum
2024-01-25
metal, music, personal

Hey, let’s talk about some musical albums I love! Please keep in mind that I’m a dilettante at best, when it comes to musical theory. My vocabulary as a non-native speaker makes this post even worse, but I thought about posting anyway, since it is my バスデイ (birthday) and I’m in a good mood :)

How often do you encounter musical albums that consist only of songs you like?

Albums are more and more a thing of the past. Artists, driven by profits resort to new strategies that help them earn more money, which is done in this age of streaming services through fighting for people’s time.

I have always been listening to songs, not albums. Most albums have some interesting tracks and tracks that I absolutely cannot stand.

This is especially true for albums that don’t have a set topic to group the songs and look like a real mess.

And then, when the stars align, the higher powers send me the gift of an album where I like each and every song.

Mastodon - Crack The Skye

YouTube Music

The first such album I ever discovered in my life was Crack The Skye by my favourite band Mastodon.

Each track in here is a unique gem:

Oblivion is the go to depression song for me. Engaging guitar play and slow lyrics - just what you need when feeling down. I also like how the lyrics overlap (the band has 3, vocalists!), although it adds complexity when trying to sing along.

Divinations - this one has more aggressive music as well as vocals, which helps change the tone from the previous tracks. But it’s good on its own too!

Quintessence - I love the intro riff so much. I could listen to it over and over; it’s that good.

The Czar: Usurper / Escape / Martyr / Spiral - the first long track on the album and probably the first long track ever that I loved. The lyrics are catchy and always make you want to sing along. Each “subtrack” is separated by an amazing riff, but they are still no match for what’s about to come in The Last Baron.

Ghost of Karelia and Crack In The Skye - these ones are just good. As simple as that, I’m really not sure what else can I say. And they also prepare you for the best track on the album:

The Last Baron - how often do you listen to 13-minute songs that leave you with the feeling that you wanted them to be longer? The lyrics are great, the music is great, the tone changes so you won’t get bored, but not too often, so you don’t get annoyed. The riffs between the parts are legendary; the 5:57 one is pretty good and the one at 8:20 became pretty famous in the guitarist community, judging by what I’ve been able to find on YouTube.

Mastodon - Once More ‘round The Sun

This album is much more mediocre than everything else here, although still much better than any other album I have listened to. Some tracks feel like fillers, but they are still very listenable.

The Motherload and Asleep in the Deep are other two other tracks that have kept me company on many days when I have felt down.

Mastodon - Emperor Of Sand

It got many tracks that give me energy to go on with my day and many that make me sing along.

Steambreather has an awesome intro riff and a really weird clip.

Jaguar God is regarded by some as the best Mastodon post-Crack in the Skye track. It transitions from slow pace to a fast one with a neat riff inbetween.

Overall, I just think that Mastodon is a great band. One indicator of its greatness is that they all sing in the songs, and they are (still) bad at it, especially live. But the songs would be ruined without their voices because they write the music in such a way that compensates fully for all their weak spots. They are literally so good at metal that they can make pretty average vocals sound unbelievably good.

Metallica - 72 Seasons

This album is the one that originally inspired me to do this post, back when I first discovered it in Autumn 2023.

The first song I discovered was Shadows Follow, which made me listen to it for days on repeat.

I was familiar with Metallica prior to listening to it, so I didn’t rush to check out the other song, because I suspected I wouldn’t like most of them. The fact that most of them are pretty long in comparison to an average modern song, does not help.

But after getting fed with Shadows Follow, I decided to give a few more tracks a try… and I liked them! So I decided to give all the other songs a try and each time I listened I felt it — the pleasant feeling of thinking to yourself “hell yeah, I’m going to listen to this a lot!”.

Conclusions?

  • I like metal
  • I like metal songs with singalongable lyrics
  • I like slower-paced songs
  • I like to have catchy riffs in the tracks I listen to
  • Mastodon is good at all of the above
